An analysis of HEATON Die 7 MACE 2 CANDEREENS coins available to view on public websites

The coin being compared was found amongst other in a box of coins from Birmingham, UK. The box has been passed through the family, and contains a number of other Heaton mint coins, as well as various 'loose change' coins dating between 1850 and 1960. We opened the box now because we're downsizing, and with a further death in the family, we needed to clear out 'stuff'.

A family albumn contains pictures with titles relating to a trip to Nanking (hear Shangai) in 1938, including pictures of Suez/Alexandria, Palestine, and HMS Bee on partol on the Yangsee river. Unfortunately the family members involved in this trip are long deceased.

The other possible provenance is members of the other side of the family being involved in engineering in Birmingham, and possibly working with the Mint.

Weight 26.95-26.99 grams

Size 39.37-39.51mm diameter, 2.48-2.56mm thick at the edge.

The coin is in by no means perfect condition. It's definately circulated, but retained most of it's attractive features.

Notable features of this Die from day 1 - visible in ALL these coins where the image resolution and lighting permits:

Around the rim of the coin:

directly out from the left seriph of A of AND, the bead is connected to the rim.

below the D of AND, three beads are connected to the rim.

Beneath the M of MACE, the rim shows indentations between the beads. However, between the E of MACE and the N of AND, these indentations are much les pronounced/absent

In the beading above PROVINCE, there is a visible line between the beads and the rim

Next to the rosette above '7', there is a distinct pattern of beads connected to the rim.
